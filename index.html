What are the Seven Wonders?

The Seven Wonders of the World are a collection of incredible architectural and artistic achievements from ancient and modern times. They represent the ingenuity, skill, and vision of humanity.

Ancient Wonders vs. New7Wonders

The original list of Seven Wonders of the Ancient World includes marvels like the Great Pyramid of Giza and the Hanging Gardens of Babylon. While most of these wonders no longer exist, they continue to inspire wonder and curiosity.In 2007, a global poll was conducted to select the New7Wonders of the World. This modern list includes iconic landmarks such as the Great Wall of China and the Taj Mahal, showcasing the enduring legacy of human creativity.

Why are They Important?

The Seven Wonders of the World are more than just historic monuments; they are symbols of human achievement and the power of imagination. They remind us of our past, inspire us in the present, and shape our future.

Great Wall of China

Location: China

Description: The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials, generally built along an east-to-west line across the historical northern borders of China to protect the Chinese states and empires against the raids and invasions of the various nomadic groups of the Eurasian Steppe.

Key Facts:

  • Construction Period: 7th century BC to 17th century AD
  • Length: Approximately 21,196 kilometers (13,171 miles)

Petra

Location: Jordan

Description: Petra is a historical and archaeological city famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system. Established possibly as early as 312 BC as the capital city of the Nabataeans, it is a symbol of Jordan as well as its most-visited tourist attraction.

Key Facts:

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1985
  • Known as the "Rose City" due to the color of the stone out of which it is carved

Christ the Redeemer

Location: Brazil

Description: Christ the Redeemer is an Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, created by French sculptor Paul Landowski and built by the Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, in collaboration with the French engineer Albert Caquot.

Key Facts:

  • Height: 30 meters (98 feet)
  • Weight: Approximately 635 metric tons (700 short tons)

Machu Picchu

Location: peru

Description: Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel located in the Eastern Cordillera of southern Peru on a 2,430-meter (7,970 ft) mountain ridge. It is located in the Machupicchu District within Urubamba Province above the Sacred Valley, which is 80 kilometers (50 mi) northwest of Cuzco.

Key Facts:

  • Built by the Inca emperor Pachacuti in the mid-15th century
  • Rediscovered by American historian Hiram Bingham in 1911

Chichen Itza

Location: Mexico

Description: Chichen Itza was a large pre-Columbian city built by the Maya people of the Terminal Classic period. The archaeological site is located in Tinúm Municipality, Yucatán State, Mexico.

Key Facts:

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1988
  • Known for its pyramid, temple, and observatory

Roman Colosseum

Location: Italy

Description: he Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an oval amphitheater in the center of the city of Rome, Italy. Built of travertine limestone, tuff, and brick-faced concrete, it was the largest amphitheater ever built at the time and held 50,000 to 80,000 spectators.

Key Facts:

  • Construction Completed: AD 80
  • Height: proximately 48 meters (157 feet)

Taj Mahal

Location: India

Description: The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1631 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Key Facts:

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983
  • Construction Period: 1632–1653

Some Facts of & Seven Wonders

Great Wall of China

Contrary to popular belief, the Great Wall of China can be seen from space with the naked eye.

Petra

Petra is also known as the "Rose City" due to the color of the stone from which it is carved.

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer is one of the New7Wonders of the World and was completed in 1931.

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu was built in the 15th century but was abandoned during the Spanish Conquest and remained hidden until its rediscovery in 1911.

Chichen Itza

The pyramid at Chichen Itza, known as El Castillo, was built with such precision that during the spring and autumn equinoxes, the setting sun casts a shadow that resembles a serpent descending the pyramid's steps.

Roman Colosseum

The Colosseum was used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, animal hunts, executions, re-enactments of famous battles, and dramas based on Classical mythology.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal changes color depending on the time of day. It appears pinkish in the morning, milky white in the evening, and golden at night when illuminated by the moon.

Historical Significance

The Seven Wonders of the World, both ancient and modern, are globally recognized for their historical significance and cultural impact. They represent the ingenuity and craftsmanship of human civilizations across different time periods and geographical locations.

Tourist Attractions

The Seven Wonders of the World are major tourist attractions, drawing millions of visitors each year. They are considered must-see destinations for travelers seeking to experience the wonders of the world firsthand and learn about their rich histories.
